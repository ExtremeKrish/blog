Influencing others is not such a difficult task. When studying How to Influence Others, keep in mind that the people you're studying are also humans, with brains that process information in the same way you do. You literally don't need a law to influence others if you know one thing: "If you want others to behave well, you must first behave well." Then you'll observe magic, and everything will be under your control. If you want others to appreciate what you're doing, start by complimenting themselves. 
Human minds are designed in such a way that we desire others to respect us. We want others to compliment us, we don't want them to expose our secrets, and we just want to be significant. Being human implies that others want to be important, that they want to be acknowledged, and that they want others to listen to them. If you look around the world, you will notice that we are all surrounded by fools and irrational people. They only want pleasure. And guess what? Here the real magic begins:
 If we, the rational humans, offer them that pleasure, that sense of importance, they will inevitably fall under our spell. They will do whatever we want, and they will blindly trust us. Now it is up to you to choose between ethical and unethical behavior. I recommend that you do ethical things for others because one day, when they realize your reality, you will be victorious, but they will end up with heartbreak. It is not recommended to create unneeded enemies since they will be the ones to spoil your plans in the future. Understanding the psychology of persuading others requires knowledge of a pleasure hormone called dopamine. This hormone is released when a person experiences pleasure, joy, or something extremely comfortable. It is released in their brains when we compliment them or allow them to talk about themselves, and when they start talking about themselves, holes in their arms open up, allowing us to manipulate them. Believe me. If you want to read humans, start with yourself. Question yourself: When do you feel joyful or sad? When do you become excited about something? What are your flaws? Who tried to manipulate you in the past, and how?
 
Remember that our motive will not be to be appreciated or respected in return, but rather to influence them.
 
Okay, let's finalize with "All we have to do is give them that social pleasure, and they will be influenced." Remember, never make a few blunders when chasing them; never do anything you would not like for yourself, such as never arguing with them, accepting their words even if you know more than them, and never criticizing them. If I sit and write about the do's and don'ts, I'll barely fill a few pages, and no one will follow these directions like robots; after all, we're all humans.
So here's the significant concept: "When you're not sure whether something is favorable or adverse, switch the characters and imagine what would happen if it happened to me. "What shall I do?" and you will undoubtedly receive your answer.
